Supreme Pizza Dippers Recipie


Overview

I have chosen to represent this dish because it is unbelievably delicious. When I was a kid I used to watch re-runs of The Chew on ABC and when I saw them make these on TV, it really inspred me to try it out for myself, and was probably one of the first times I took a recipie off the internet and made it for myself. Nostalga aside, they make a really great snack.



Ingredients

  • 1/2 Pound of Hot Italian Sausage
  • 1/2 Pound of Pepperoni
  • Kosher Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • 2 Lbs. of Pizza Dough


Directions

  1. Place a 10 inch cast iron pan in an oven and preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. In a small bowl, stir together 1/2 cup of the parmesan cheese with the oregano, parsley and season with salt and pepper.
  3. In a medium sauté pan heat a few tablespoons of olive oil over medium. Add the sausage and cook until crispy, breaking up with a wooden spoon as it cooks. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate and allow to cool.
  4. Add the bell peppers to the pan and sauté for 2 minutes just to soften. Use a slotted spoon and transfer to a plate.
  5. In the same pan with the sausage drippings remaining, add the meatballs and cook on all sides until dark golden brown and cooked through, about 10 minutes. Remove to a paper towel-lined plate and allow to cool.
  6. Toss the filling ingredients plus shredded mozzarella together to combine.
  7. On a lightly floured surface, with a floured rolling pin, roll out dough to 1/2 inch thickness. Use a floured 3 inch biscuit cutter to cut circles out of the dough.
  8. Place a small amount of filling into the center of each circle.
  9. Once all the dough circles are topped with filling begin to pinch the edges of each circle together to form filled balls of dough.
  10. Remove the hot pan from the oven and drizzle with a few tablespoons of olive oil. Place the balls seam side down into the pan. Brush the top with olive oil and sprinkle with the cheese mixture.
  11. Bake for 15 minutes or until deep golden brown on top.
  12. Remove from oven and allow to cool for a few minutes before serving.
  13. Garnish with the remaining parmesan cheese, anchovies and warm marinara sauce for dipping.
